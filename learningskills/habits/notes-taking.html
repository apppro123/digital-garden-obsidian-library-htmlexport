Notes taking

Take notes and create notes by asking yourself questions about the usefulness of the taken notes and how you can connect this new thing to other learned things. Create and use to remember it.

TIP for better notes

  • Think: Even before you have to take notes, e.g. before a lecture or a book, be clear what you want to get out, what is your aim with the notes later.
  • Identify: During the notes taking process, find out what is (most) helpful for you aim.
  • Prioritise: After go over your notes again and highlight the most important things or summarise them.

Aim of note taking

Why do you take notes? That is an important questions because the answer determines also the approach. In most cases we want to remember the information for a long time. But it is also very likely that we won't remember everything just after gaining the information for the first time and taking notes. So we also have to take notes in a way that we can later review them and learn or better remember more of it.
So we take notes to group and connect the new information in a way we understand them better. And to have a fundament for further learning.

Part the Notes taking process

Divide e.g. your sheet into two columns. On the one side just take notes. On the other side get creative and create notes yourself connected to your taken notes.

Take notes

Best practice is to group notes by subject. If there are sub-subjects just indent.
Don't transcript what you e.g. hear. That is not efficient for remembering or using them later. Prioritise.

Make own abbreviations

Learning a complex but high efficient abbreviations system could take too long and would be another challenge. By creating your own ones, you can always adapt what you abbreviate and create them on your own.
For a topic where a long word e.g. artificial intelligent => AI is used often you can make your own abbreviation and just put a post it (or write it down at the beginning or end of this section) with the abbreviation on your notes to remember what it meant later on.

Create notes

In the create notes column, you should deepen the learned things.
Not limited but standard questions for creating notes can be:

  • How can I use this?
  • Why must I use this?
  • When will I use this?

When to take notes?

It is better to not take (too much) notes while listening or reading. You don't and should not write everything down, but just enough to remember when it is a presentation, or even pause the video/audiobook or close the book before taking notes.
That way you really focus on the information input first and are not disctracted by note taking. Afterwards you do active recalling and your brain has to review/remember it again. This is especially important for getting it to the long term memory.

Later/After it

After the e.g. lecture or section of book review your notes, because the memory of it is still fresh and maybe you want to add or change some things.

Practice

Even take and create notes on fictional topics to keep you practice this skill and test your comprehenions on reading/listening/...

Medium

There are two main approaches:

  • By hand you can take notes more freely and e.g. draw things.
  • By typing (e.g. on a laptop) you are (probably) faster and write nicer.
    Today we have the opportunity to have the best of two worlds with a tablet.
