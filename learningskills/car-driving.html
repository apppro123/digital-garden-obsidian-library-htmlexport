changing directions

Care for

Some things are quite obvious/clear in retroperspective, but some people still do it (don't know to say names here^^)

security lines

You are not allowed to cross them. Image these as walls or spikes. You wouldn't drive in or on such things either.

  • security lines at crossroads/light signals

Use a (side) street

The two cases I learnt are using it for turning or driving backwards in such a street.
In both cases be very aware of your surroundings and what's going on behind you.

Turn

  • drive backwards on the side of the opposing traffic bc.
    1. we won't stay there long (just turn), so it's not a problem
    2. we are immediately on the right side, to leave the (side) street again on the right side

backwards in

  • drive backwards on the side, where you would also enter when driving ahead bc.
    • we use this street for longer
    • don't bother the opposing traffic (otherwise, they would have to drive backwards, too)

Others

  • don't go over triangles (no precedence signals on the road)
  • stay on your road line

just one way

If there is just one way, for all vehicles

  • stay on your side e.g. don't use the bike road

multiple ways

e.g. straight on and right

  • completely close the gap to the side you want to go e.g. also the bike road (so there is no )

before lightning signals

  • wait on the side, you won't to go
  • don't use the bike road, yet

Park

correct

The rear wheels cannot be moved anymore. So if the rear is not good yet, correct it first

go nearer

  1. go backwards as near as possible on the side you park in (with the rear wheels).
  2. Go forward away a little bit
  3. backwards again
