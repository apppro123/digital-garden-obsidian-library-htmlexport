Safety net

#remarkN For me it is quite similar to Start with why
Why do you want to learn this? With the following questions, you know what to expect after you learned this. What can and cannot you do with it? So you're not disappointed.

Ask yourself questions

  • Context: What are we talking about?
  • Importancy:
    • Why is this important?
    • How important is this?
  • Connections & Limitations
    • What can I do when I learn this?
    • What can't I do even when I learnt this?
  • Further knowledge
    - Where I can find further knowledge?
    - Who are the experts?
    #remarkN Related to the note taking technique from Limitless. There you also have a second column, where you should take notes on the broader topic like "How can I use this" and also "Why must I use this?".

Emotions

It's also really good, if you can connect information to emotions. #remarkN So it is even more important to connect information and ask, where it fits. Thus you touch on more subjects and have a higher chance on finding connection to subjects your are passionate about.

In lectures

Not taking notes, but building the foundation for the specifics later on.
So also there ask (maybe stupid sounding) questions like

  • So we are talking about ...?
  • So you are saying ...?
  • That means if ... happens than ... is the consequence?

Core basics

  • concepts
  • maybe things, experts don't even say anymore
  • How far do they get me? e.g. where can('t) I apply them?
  • What affects my learning here the most?
  • How fast can I learn it?

Focus

Focus deep on the basics progress:

  • identify a core skill
  • test the core skill in different contexts
  • find where i can’t use the core skill (basically the limits)
    Strong core basics means better and faster learning later.

Information categories

  1. Safety net: The logical backbone: Everything relies on this.
  2. Core basics: Significant and Immediate (everything else builds on it)
  3. Admin: hard (for me), less important
  4. Good for you: don't care, unimportant
    E.g. if I'm better at conceptualising but not with names and numbers, then first learn concepts and put names and numbers into Admin or even Good for you

Jump around & learn longer

Learn things in the order you are interested in, not in the order they aregiven. That way it's easier to learn longer and stay motivated. Due to the basics you still understand everything, even though you maybe don't follow the given way. Otherwise you burn your motiviation and quit quite fast again.
#example In a book go to chapter 9 first, if that is the most intersting one to you and then maybe chapter 2.
