general

What banks (can) do with your money

Banks don't invest your money directly but rather need it as safety to get more money from the central/national bank, to invest that in e.g. houses, fonds, stocks.

climate crisis is not solvable in todays finacial system

  • according to Turiq Fancy, former Investment banker and head sustainability at Blackrock
  • system is to blame, not workers: System needs all companies to be as profitable as possible. It doesn't care about sustainability of the world. And green washing is still far more favourable.

ESG is a lie!

ESG = Environment, Social, Governance
#article Bloomberg: The ESG Mirage

  • At least for a lot of companies and most of the big ones.
  • It's not about the impact of the company on the environment but rather the other way around: What an impact the environment has on companies and its investors.
  • example
    • Mac Donalds has emitted more, but because that is not a danger to the company, it didn't reflect in the ESG rating. But when it introduced more recycling in some countries, where it had to do so in the next years anyways, its ESG score went up.
    • "water stress" is one category of Environment
      • it measures if the region has enough water for the company

Mythen

"It doesn't matter, where my money lies."

Not true, because

  • you show with which values (e.g. climate strategy) of a bank/institution you (dis)agree

Banks want to keep you long term

  • they give better conditions for younger people
  • want to earn off of you (logically) e.g. with credits

"Sustainable investing is not worth it."

Not true. There are the same risks as conventional investing.

  • A lot of companies will go bankrupt.

"Sustainable investments are not sustainable for the planet"

Today, (mostly) true. Green washing is in most cases cheaper/easier

In many cases it's cheaper and easiert to market yourself as green
- Tariq Fancy

company impact vs investor impact

  • company maybe transitions to be more sustainable without the need of investments
  • maybe company just uses 10% (that would be through investments) for the new facality and funds 90% themselves

make brown company greener

  • voting for more sustainability in brown companies can have a greater impact
    • maybe a green company just gets a little bit greener through investments, but a brown company could (will probably) have more potential for sustainability

"My investment has no impact anyways."

Mostly true when done the conventional way. And if you don't have a lot of money.

But the most impactful ways would be

  • turn brown into green
    • make a brown company green through voting
    • good idea is through a Fond, which actively votes for more sustainability with its money
  • help Startups
    • can have a big impact (because without investment, it would have not been possible at all)
    • very risky (if company fails everything is gone) and possibly not a big ROI
  • Investing in oneself
    • invest in the own house, garden, clothes, meals
      • e.g. solar panels with the rising electricity prices it is worthy
    • support local projects
  • Pressure on politics or society
    • through NGOs e.g. Greenpeace
