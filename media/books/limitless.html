Neuronal plasticity

  • The brain is growing and neurons make new connections all the time.
  • Learning means making new connections between neurons.
  • Remembering is strengthening a connection.
    We can change our brain and shape it how we want.

100% capacitiy

We always use 100% of our brain. Even when sleeping 100% is used. Otherwise it would be very inefficient, at least it uses the most energy as one organ.

Gut feeling

We have a second brain in our digestive tract. So there is something like a gut feeling!

  • This has around 10^8 neurons.
  • It is part of the enteric nervous system and is connected to the neuronal system by the veganus nerve.

(Hear) With your whole brain

Brain can process around 400 words/minute. but we normally talk with a speed of 100 w/m that means you don't focus just on hearing or more specific 3/4 of the brain can get/be distracted. The same problem is with reading so prefer Speed reading

HEAR methode

  • Halt: full focus on the topic, block all distractions (or like while meditating just shift your focus back, if you notice your got distracted) Focus
  • Empathy: understand speaking person
  • Anticipate: connect information with information, before the best is (positive) anticipation Think positive
  • Recapitulate: Remember and take notes Notes taking

Connect information

Every time you connect an information with another one, you recall and therefor strengthen the memory. The more connections the better you remember something.

  • Location: Connect information with a location. Great memory artists connect information with things/locations in a familiar place e.g. their apartment.
  • Emotions: Connect an emotion to an information.
  • Tell a story/visualize: Wrapping an information in a story, doesn't have to be realistic, and visualize it, is a very useful (and funny, there is an emotion ;) ) way to strengthen a memory.

Baker/baker paradox

Humans are more likely to remember that a person is a baker then if his name is Baker. This is due to more connections are made with the job of a baker (picture, smell, location of a bakery, bread, ...) compared to one person named Baker.
