Story and seeing the positive in stress

  • very overwhelmed, a lot of stress => bad for physical and social life
  • recognise that stress doesn't have to be enemy => see stressful situations as opportunities:
    - potential benefits of stress: enhanced focus, heightened energy
    - altering perspective on it can already have positive effects
    TLDR;
    When she started a new job at a technology company, Laurie felt overwhelmed. The stress of working remotely, and constant worries about job security and performance weighed her down. Her annual review loomed large in her mind, exacerbating her stress to the point where it began to affect her physical health and social life. Laurie’s experience reflects a psychological theory – proposed by Richard Lazarus and Susan Folkman – which describes stress as a reaction to a perceived threat that surpasses your coping resources. It’s important to differentiate between stress, which typically arises from specific external pressures and is often short-term, and anxiety, which is a more generalised response that can persist even in the absence of external stressors.

Laurie’s experience illustrates a common scenario: seeing stress as an insurmountable threat. But the first step to befriending stress is recognising that it doesn’t have to be your enemy. Stress can be a motivator, a signal to engage with challenges in ways that promote growth and resilience. By reframing stressful situations as opportunities, you can transform your stress into action. This reframing involves actively choosing to see the potential benefits of stress, such as enhanced focus and heightened energy. For instance, Laurie set small, achievable goals like preparing one section of an important presentation each day, which channeled her anxious energy into productive tasks and utilised her body’s natural stress response to enhance performance, rather than hinder it. Research indicates that viewing stress as a potentially positive challenge can significantly alter physiological responses, reducing negative moods and improving cognitive function.

Approaches to enhancing personal resilience

  • improve emotional stability through breathing and scheduling time for oneself
  • ADHD and stress
    - healthier coping strategies than (just) medications: structured daily routing, engaging in regular exercise, setting achievable goals to improve his focus
    - especially structured routines and physical activity can greatly reduce symptoms of ADHD and enhance general well-being
    TLDR;
    Cameron’s experience illustrates the risks of adopting unhealthy coping mechanisms. He struggled with substance dependence as a means to cope with ADHD and job-related stress. Initially relying on medications like Klonopin and recreational cannabis, Cameron soon realized these were only temporary fixes that didn’t address the root causes of his stress. Encouraged by his therapist, Cameron explored healthier strategies such as establishing a structured daily routine, engaging in regular exercise, and setting achievable goals to improve his focus. These changes not only decreased his reliance on substances but also enhanced his overall life satisfaction by fostering a sense of accomplishment and autonomy. Creating a routine for yourself could involve simple steps like waking up at the same time each day, or integrating a short walk into your lunch break. Structured routines and physical activity can greatly reduce symptoms of ADHD and enhance general well-being.
  • combat chronic pain by focusing on what truly matters e.g. from physical discomfort to own values and aspirations e.g. doing activities you like/value and thus change day-to-day experience

Stress resets

  • mind resets
    • thoughts are a mess? get back to NOW and HERE by noticing what your feelings, thoughts and actions are, assess if/how they align with your long-term goals
    • indecisive? think about cons/pros, different long-/short-term scenarios and their benefits/drawbacks
    • intense emotions? notice them and recognise their temporary nature
  • body resets
    • cold wate: face in large ice water bowl for 60 seconds to active vagus nerve => slows heart heart, refocuses on mind
      • especially useful when combined with intense exercise, muscle relaxation, and paced breathing.
    • anxious or frustrated? engage in short bursts of physical activity like squats, push-ups, and jumping jacks for a set duration => intense workout dispels anxious energy and improves mood and cognitive function, effectively reducing stress and helping you focus away from the pressures at hand
    • to get comfort place one hand over your heart and the other on your belly, taking deep breaths
      • this can mimic the calming effects of a hug, significantly lowering your heart rate and cortisol levels
      • easy gesture of self-compassion and always available (to reduce stress)
  • behavioural resets
    • STOP-method
      • remind yourself: calm down, take a step back, observe feelings/surrounding and proceed mindfully
      • physical note can help
      • encourages self-reflection and improves self-management and healthier habits
    • stuck in a loop? Go for a walk where you can focus on your (natural) environment and be fully present
      • boosts your mental clarity and creativity but also contributes to your physical health, reducing risks of diseases and enhancing overall well-being.
    • use a hope kit filled with items and activities that uplift you, such as inspiring photos, your favorite music, or meaningful mementos
      • Regularly engaging with your collection will help you combat feelings of hopelessness and negativity
      • physical box or digital collection like the Virtual Hope Box app

Stress buffers

  • mind buffers
    • combat old, negative beliefs by noticing when and how they effect oneself => then challenge these thought by acting contrary to them
      • e.g. if you believe your unworthy, try accepting compliments
    • worried about things outside of own control you can set aside times to address these thoughts
      • helps prevent worry from overtaking everyday life
    • identify positives around you by routinely recognising good things around
  • body buffers
    • interoceptive exposure: face panic attacks head-on by safely inducing and embracing panic-like symptoms in controlled settings e.g. hyperventilating
      • helps reduce fear by familiarising with it
    • regular exercises by setting [[SMART goal setting|SMART] goals like running or group fitness and committing to it
      • Regular physical activity significantly boosts mood, focus, and overall health, helping you tackle other life challenges more effectively
    • create humour daily: engage with people you make you laugh, revisit funny content and try improv classes
      • enhances connections, reduces stress and improves cardiovascular health
  • behaviour buffers
    • never be lonely again ( #disclaimerN okay, maybe this claim doesn't really hold^^) by smiling, making eye contact and doing small talk
      • mitigating the stress of feeling isolated, and enhancing your sense of belonging.
    • actively schedule time for enjoyable activities e.g. spend time with friends, doing a hobby
    • analyse mistakes using chain analysis: identify factors leading to error and devise actionable steps to prevent recurrence
