from 71 murders to anti-criminal

Take a moment to reflect on the tale of Pedro Rodrigues Filho, a man born into brutality, bearing a dented skull from his father's beatings. Rodrigues Filho, who died in 2023, was a serial killer who had murdered 71 people by 1985. Yet his story didn't end in a prison cell. He started working out, learned to read and write, and in 2007, thanks to a legal loophole, walked out of prison a free man. He began waking up at 4 a.m. daily, abstained from drugs and alcohol, shared inspirational stories on YouTube, and counseled young criminals, swearing that he was repulsed by his former self and considering himself reborn.
So can we change ourselves fundamentally?

Future self

  • Our perception of our future selves profoundly influences our current decisions
  • think of our future self as an extension of your present self or even a good friend (not as a stranger)
    • then we are more likely to make good decisions (than we would for a stranger)
  • A day in the present feels longer than the anticipation of a day spent on a task six months from now.
  • If we don't actively focus on our future selves, we risk making decisions in the present that could lead to future regret. So perhaps it's time to weigh anchor and let our future selves guide us as we navigate the sea of choices that life presents.
  • interesting: more patience, when immediacy of present is removed
    • options: $1000 in six months or $990 right now => most choose $990 now
    • options: $900 in a year or $1000 in eighteen months => most people choose $1000

Get closer

  • compose a letter or time capsule to make future self more tangible
  • visualise your future, and then mentally travel back to the present => take concrete steps
    • contemplating the future, use days instead of years as your unit of time – a perspective that's easier to comprehend and operationalise

yes/damn effect

We struggle to imagine our future selves vividly, therefor we make bad decisions and:

  • procrastinate
  • say yes, but then damn: have you ever agreed to something scheduled for a future time, only to regret it when that time came

Unhelpful biases

  • projection bias – our tendency to assume our future selves will feel the way we currently do
  • end-of-history-illusion – our belief that our personality and preferences won't change much in the future

Better decisions

  • instant punishments ( #remarkN that sounds a little too harsh and I'm not a fan of punishments anyways :) )
  • make present more enoyable
    • e.g. connect nice things to otherwise unappealing tasks
  • break down difficult goals
