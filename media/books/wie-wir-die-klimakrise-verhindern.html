Facts

  • 2019: 51 Bil. tonnes CO2e
    • 37 bil. just CO2, 10 bil. just carbon
  • 1 bil. people no access to electricity
  • income per capita is heavily linked to energy consumption

Different sectors

  • Industry: 31%
  • Electricity: 27%
  • Agriculture: 19%
  • Transport: 16%
  • Cool/Heat: 7%

Energy consumption

  • world: 3000 gigawatt
  • USA: 500 gigawatt
  • middle-size city: 1 gigawatt
  • little city: 1 megawatt
  • house: 1 kilowatt

Greenhouse gases

Not CO2 gases

Other gases like CH4 and NOx (nitrogen oxides) are much worse than CO2.

Agriculture

Less crop yield globally (regional very different).

  • 50 bil. USD/y are in danger alone in USA (heat incompatible)

More heat

  • Death by heat more likey
    • especially with higher humidity => cannot sweat anymore
  • regional big differences: flood <=> heat
  • moscitos and other insects are more likely
    • better spread of illnesses

Examples

Conflict

#example Syria: biggest drought 2007-2013 => a lot of people moved to the cities => rise of rebellion

Agrictulture

#example farmer in America: financially very good

  1. heat resistent crop, cattle
  2. "century"-flooding/rain (today rather every 10 years)
    • dam breaks
    • crops are flooded away
    • cattle no food, because all stock is wet
    • streets are blocked

Wealth

Living standard of global population increases

  • more emissions (correlation with welath)
  • until 2050 +50% energy consumption
  • next 40 years size of NYC will be built

Help

Rich countries have to help the poor ( #remarkN DebtForClimate) to reach wealth without emissions.

Energy

Adaption to new energy sources is slow

  • needs at least centuries (impulse by price not environment)
  • we now need very fast transition
    • politics and technic are needed
  • energy industry is sedate
    • huge (5 trillion/y)
    • much rules
    • long lasting (e.g. building of power plant)
    • investions are expensive
    • no mistakes tolerable

Production factors

  • land usage/power density

Eco addition

  • but has to be affordable for the whole world
  • first implement option with lowest eco-addition
    • if not done, then there are other reasons than the price
  • focus research on eareas with high eco addition
    • aim to get all countries to participate
  • measures how difficult it is to get to 0
    • methods/things that have no CO2-neutral alternative yet (e.g. cement), we can take prices of Direct Air Capture
      • DAC costs today around 200$/tonne CO2, we should be able to cut this down to 100$/tonne
      • COVID lockdown cost the 25-40 times more than the 100$/tonne
  • yearly: 5.1 trillion $/y or 6% of global BIP which is huge, but still much cheaper than shutting off certain economic sectors ( #remarkN But maybe we can at least think about slowing down social/environmental harming sectors, especially if we have alternative or they are just luxury like the whole luxury sector^^)
