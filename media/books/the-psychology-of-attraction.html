Read personalities

CANOE or OCEAN


The Big Five Model, also known as the Five-Factor Model, is the most widely accepted personality theory held by psychologists today. The theory states that personality can be boiled down to five core factors, known by the acronym CANOE or OCEAN.

  • Openness to Experience – prefers routine, practical vs. imaginative, spontaneous
  • Conscientiousness – impulsive, disorganised vs. disciplined, careful
  • Extraversion – reserved, thoughtful vs. sociable, fun-loving
  • Agreeableness – suspicious, uncooperative vs. trusting, helpful
  • Neuroticism – calm, confident vs. anxious, pessimistic
    Big Five Personality Traits: The 5-Factor Model of Personality

Primary value

The Resource theory by Foa is about 6 different goods:

  • Love: affection, acceptance, sympathy
  • Service: help, caring, security
  • Status: responsibility, praise and title making proud
  • Money: coins, paying methods and other valuable things
  • Goods: products, objects and materials
  • Information: advise, suggestions, opinions and instructions
    Decision of a human make sense for them, not for us.

Request

Justify (as in a court)

Justifying your request, even if it doesn't make sense, is crucial. Humans like having a reason, even if it's trivial, for doing something e.g. "Can I go in front you to print. I'm in a hurry."

because

Always use the word "because", that's key in justifying your request.

be direct

Speak directly to humans, so it's more likely, they will say "yes".

Ability > Activity

  • Ask "Who can write nicely?" instead of "I need somebody who will write down the things."
  • Praise somebody for somethings and then ask the for the matching To-Do: "I really like your hand-writing. Could you please finish up the cards?"

Mistakes are sexy

Okay, maybe that's a little exaggerated. But at least vulnerability is sexy, because mistakes make us seem more human. Because we all do mistakes and we like people, that are like us, we like people that have mistakes/admit their mistakes.

Vulnerability sounds like truth and feels like courage.
- Brené Brown

  • Everybody has weaknesses: The right people will like your exactly because of them!

Benjamin Franklin effect

  • Ask for something e.g. advice, show weakness/vulnerability, brings people together.
  • But just ask for things, that your really like.

Not perfection

In life it's not about perfection, it's about doing your thing.

Upset

How to best cope situations/humans that are (very) upset.

  1. Designate/Name their feelings e.g. by repeating their feelings/words they use to express them
  2. show Understanding by asking questions e.g. "How can I help?"

Know that

  • you cannot speak against feelings, but you can recognise/acknowledge them.
  • staying calm doesn't help a lot but maybe even makes the other person more upset/angry/emotional

Set fire

Okay, that sounds a little too dramatic. In essence it's just about saying "no" and "yes" in the right situations and why saying "no" is really important!

  • Limited amount of time/resources also means (in a world of nearly unlimited possibilities and connections): Saying "no" to "bad" relationships creates times for "good" relationships.
  • #analogy Sometimes fields have to be burnt down, to create place for healthy plants.
