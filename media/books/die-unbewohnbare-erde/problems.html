known problems on a heating planet

Rising sea level

  • between 1.2m and 2.5m (with a "medium" heat increase)
  • examples which will be under water until 2050: all beaches, Kennedy Space center, Jakarte (one of the fastest growing cities, today at 10 million)
  • today there are already 4x more flooding than 1980, and 2x compared to 2004
  • East cost of America will be flooded "every second day" with middle-low temperature increase
  • nearly 2/3 of the cities are located at costs => in danger of flooding
  • Albedo effect less ice means even more heat...
  • end of sea level increase could be at 50-80 metres (over centuries)
  • short notes last update
    • Übersicht versch. Szenarien und Zeiten der NASA vom IPCC (Weltklimarat) Bericht 6: https://sealevel.nasa.gov/ipcc-ar6-sea-level-projection-tool
    • Wikipedia-Artikel dazu https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_level_rise sagt: ein paar Meter sind (gut) möglich; bis 2050 vermutlich ca. 150 Millionen unter Wasser bei Flut und 300 Millionen jährlich überschwemmt (das sind 40 bzw. 50 Mio. mehr als 2010 und auch die Bevölkerungsanzahl ist von 2010 ohne Bevölkerungsanstieg in die Rechnung aufgenommen zu haben).

wild fires

  • wild fires have even hit and burn down cities, our modern castles
  • fires in the north are resulting in ash on the ice, which has a darker surface as a result and so reflects less heat
  • increase by a factor of 4 per one degree increase in temperature

forest loss

  • Bolsonaros cutting policies can result in plus 13 gigatons of emissions until 2030
  • forest floors can absorb 77% less methane in the last 3 decades
  • one square kilometre of forest results in 27 more cases of Malaria
    • #remarkN in the last decade the world lost around 10 million hectares per year, 4.7 million net loss per year #source #ourworldindata How much deforestation occurs each year?

fresh water

  • makes up 0.007% of all water
  • unfair distribution will just increase due to climate crisis
  • cities are the most vulnerable ( #remarkN the place where most of the people (will) live)
  • due to shortage, there will be more conflicts and crisis
  • more and deeper groundwater is used, which was brought there over millions of years
    • this will not fill up any time soon, so there will be even more problems once we used it
  • crisis just because of politics, so it can also be solved by politics

absorption through seas

  • 1/4 of the human produced CO2
  • 90% of the excessive heat in the last 50 years

addification

  • coral bleaching
    • dying out due to disappearing of zoonosis
  • oxygen poor zones
    • often caused to to excessive fertilizing
  • slowing streams down (global flat conveyor) e.g. Gulf stream

air pollution

  • 930ppm results in 21% less cognitive ability
    • e.g. in class rooms we have already around 1000ppm
  • leaded materials have caused in more criminality and disabilities over the last 50 years
  • more physical sickness and dementia
  • 98% of industrialized cities have bad air according to WHO
  • in total 95% of people breath bad air daily
  • every 6th death is caused by bad air

plastic

  • more and more Micro- /Macroplastic in our food and water ((eco)systems)

diseases

  • through climate shifts, more carriers can populate wider regions and therefore effect more people
    • carriers like mosquito, fleas, ticks
  • globalisation helps in spreading
  • old illnesses get exposed by melting ice (floors)
  • even "friendly" bacteria/viruses can become harming with increases temperature
    • around 99% of bacteria in our body are unknown to science

economy

  • with heating climate, every degree more results in one percent less economic growth
  • -23% economic growth until 2100
    • in comparison, the 1930 economic crisis resulted in 15% reduction
  • optimal temperature average is 15 degree Celsius for most economic efficiency
    • "by accident" the averages of the biggest national economies like the USA
  • unbalanced distribution of disadvantages, BUT the decline of one economy always has an effect on (all) others, too

conflicts

  • per half degree, probability of an armed conflict rises by 10-20%
  • the exploitation/conflicts in the east got even more due to fossil fuels => more heat => more conflicts
  • wars are often caused due to a lack of materials, exactly what climate change and a rising population are bringing us to

psyche

  • more depression, psychical illnesses and suicides with increasing temperatures
  • (especially young people) much strong traumatic disruptions following a natural catastrophe

unknown unknowns

All previous listed problems are "known knowns".
