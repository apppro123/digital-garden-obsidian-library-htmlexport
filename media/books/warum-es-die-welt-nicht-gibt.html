general notes

  • most things are facts: they also exist without us
  • science is a common good in general
  • human is looking for a subject which he can imply that it "knows" (everything or could know everything)
  • human mid can destroy itself: just assuming that we are a Bio-/meatmachine, which has a awareness cinema

Sense = kind of being given (Art des Gegebenseins)

  • is objective, even though there are multipls aspects/views
    • e.g. a mountain looks different from different sites, but it is still objective how it looks from the different places

attitude towards sth.

  • e.g. dog <-> tyke (both is a dog, but the second is negative)

Registry (Registratur)

  • certain selection of methods and and requirements/assumptions/circumstances

philosophical positions

Postmodernism

  • before "new realisms"
  • assumption that we can never see the world correct
    • like multiple glasses (science, politics, poetry, ...)
  • general form of constructivism
    • e.g. Emmanuel Kant
    • "green glasses"

Metaphysics

  • invented "world", because she wanted to describe "reality"
  • also called "old realism"

Constructivism

  • everything is imagination

New Realism

  • There is everything BUT "the world"
  • there are a lot of different world, but not an all-encompassing one
    • There is no theory of everything

Pluralism

  • multiple substances/levels
  • philosophical medium of characteristics

Monism

  • assumes "Oneness" (Wiki) or a "Superobject" (book)

Dualism

  • could be true but is ungrounded (unbegründet): Why shouldn't there be more?

Materialism/science

  • false bc. it also suggest a (the) world
  • and e.g. a living being is not (just) identical with its particles, otherwise "it" would have existed the whole time the particles existed
    • (my annotation) But what is with the constallation in addition? And maybe the powers between the particles? Because particles change the whole time, don't they?
  • BUT in the "universe" science there are scientific facts for sure!
  • based on a distorted reality
    • thinks that all perception rely on building hypotheses and to be experimentially proven
    • but human and his understanding of mind doesn't exists in the "universe" universe but needs the mind and sense => that needs to be encrypted by daily communication
  • fetishism of science: we think that an expert panel knows everything and should/could/can decide everything about our cohabition

difference between human and animal

  • human isn't just what he is, but also searches for what he is

art

  • meaning of it is to show the ambivalence => a thing appears together with its sense in a "universe"

New Realism (the book is mainly about that)

different universes of discourse

examples
- universe for physics: everything with natural laws, but e.g. not the living room
- natural numbers: arithmetic rules, ...
- political scope with voters, politicians, ...

ontological reduction (get back to what is real)

  • analyse an scope and reduce it => maybe it is just a talking scope ("Redebereich")
  • maybe it has a false assumption due to a historical, psychological, economic, ... randomness

Existence

  • appearance in a "universe"
  • existence relates to one/some universes but never to all of, so it is relative
    - same with existantial statements
    => World cannot "exist" but everything exists in it!

(New) Reality

  • exists of things and their appearances
