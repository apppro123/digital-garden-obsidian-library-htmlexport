Good habits to establish

#book 12 Rules for Life - An antidote to chaos
#author Jordan B. Peterson
tags: #habits #psychology
overview:

Fixed sleep schedule

A fixed sleep schedule and a good breakfast is very important. This can already solve a lot of mental ilnesses.
I think it is in general about having a schedule and structure in daily life.

change your posture, change your life

Standing straight, shoulders back makes you feel more confident. Look forward (not down) and walk tall.

Smile all day long :)

Smiling also makes happier and so healthier.

Talk to people

Express your desires and opinion. Just then you can get positive feedback and others know how you feel. More likely good things will happen.
And if you get "bad" feedback, you can learn from it. But I think most often people are in 1 on 1 situations nice and rather want to please. That can be bad also, if you don't get critical feedback at all.

Treat yourself as you would treat sb. you are responsible for

Often we treat ourselves worse than we treat our family or strangers. We have far higher expectations and punish ourselves (at least with thoughts) far more often.
A little similar to "Just do to others, what you would like to be done to you, too.".

Make friends with ppl. who want the best for you

Aim carefully

What you aim at determines what you see. Your attitude has a big impact on the result.
An example with eyes: They can focus just on some few things, others are peripheral with low resolution. So use every tool wisely and now what to "look for".
#video Basketball Awareness Test with bear walking through

Aim at a better life for everybody

Don't define "better" because totalitarianism is bad. It should and will change over time and manifest in different actions.

Three questions to take better actions

Ask

  1. What bothers me?
  2. Could I fix (a part of) it?
  3. Would I fix (a part of) it?
    What are you waiting for, do it!

Aim at the highest good

Step by step and day by day improve yourself.
Then you are less concerned about others (actions) because you know you have enough to do for yourself.
Compare yourself to who you were yesterday. (And not to others)

Tell the truth or at least don't lie.

If you always say yes (also to little things you dislike), you cannot say no at a moment when it matters the most.

new information

A new information can destroy old beliefs and challenge us before it is reborn as something better. Every bit of learning is a little death.
Assume that the poerson you are listening to knows something you don't.

think to prevent

memory is a tool to prevent negative events happening again in the future. To learn from the past.

listen to understand

Improve listening and undestanding of others.
After sb. spoke try to summarize it and ask if that's right. If not improve until the other person aggrees on it.

in crisis; set time windows

In a crisis just think about it in scheduled time, so you don't get overwhelmed.
With that you can also preserve energy. Crisis is not one battle, it's a long lasting war.
Schedule the time during the day, not before sleeping (otherwise you cannot sleep well).

See the good in the being

Pet a cat when there is one on the street as an example. And strive for improving everything around you by improving yourself :)

Nature Facts

Social hierarchy is very old and born

In an unstable world, short-term success is desirable. Because you don't know if the situation chances at once, so you rather take whatever you can if possible.
In comparison in a stable world long-term thinking is better.

Shame on you ;)

Shame is natural and comes with self-consciousness. The ideal (e.g. person with the perfect body) shames everyone else. But also death shames the living.
Nudity is especially shameful because then one is very vulnerable and everyone can see ones short comings.

Reality of suffering

is not doubtable.

evil

Each human being has an immense capacity of evil.
Worst sin: Torment of others for the sake of it. And the good is whatever strive to stop it/such things.
BUT if there is something not good, there has to be good things, too.

fundamental moral conclusion

  • Aim high, pay attention
  • fix what you can fix
  • Don't be arrogant in your knowledge
  • Become aware of your own insufficiency e.g. malevolence
  • Say the truth or at least don't lie!
  • To the best of my ability I will act in a manner that leads to the alleviation of unnecessary pain and suffering.

Bad event, chaos and our being

When something bad happens (e.g. our car breaks down or our partner is not honest) we not only are confronted with chaos, but we also question ourselves.
Chaos grows over time until it breaks out tremendously. So we have to try to fix it at any time and don't ignore even the little things.

household/-care

Set your house in perfect order before you criticise the world

  • Say only those things that make you strong.
  • Start to stop doing what you know to be wrong.
  • Fix what you can and the rest will be bearable.

change the future

If you are disciplined and privilege the future over the present you can change the structure of reality.

Don't let your child do anything, that makes you dislike them.

Take responsibility

  • for their discipline.
  • and your own boundaries/limits. So you don't react badly, because of something else.

Use minimum necessary force.

Equality

men loose against women every time

Men loose when competing against women anyways because

  • if they win, it's clear/they should have won and maybe lose in status because the have beaten a woman and are too dominant.
  • if they lose it's a shame
    So they can only really compete against men to "win".

Equal opportunities but not equal outcome

Collective pursuit always produces winners and losers.
The pursuit of goals is one measuring of life. That creates hierarchy. Otherwise, if there would be always the same outcome for everybody, it is not worth pursuing.
