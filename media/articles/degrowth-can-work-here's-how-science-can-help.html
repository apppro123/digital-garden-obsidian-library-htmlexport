Summary

Wealthy countries can create prosperity while using less materials and energy if they abandon economic growth as an objective. Polls suggests that a lot people prioritize e.g. ecological values, less working hours over GDP.
What we need? More research in all disciplines towards degrowth and ecological economics and for that it needs more funding, attention on political agendas and debating.

  • IPCC and Intergovernmental Science-Poly Platform on biodiversity and Ecosystem Services (IPBES) suggest that degrowth policies should be considered in the fight against climate breakdown and biodiversity loss, respectively.

Tips

  • Reduce less-necessary production
    • fossil fuels sector
    • mass-produced meat and dairy
    • fast fashion
    • advertising
    • cars
    • aviation (private jets)
  • Improve public services
    • universal access to high-quality health care
    • education
    • housing
    • transportation
    • Internet
    • renewable energy
    • nutritious food
  • Introduce green jobs guarantee: Train/mobilize labour around urgent sozial/ecological objectives
    • installing renewables
    • insulating buildings
    • regenerating ecosystems
    • improving social care
  • Reduce working time
    • lowering retiement age
    • encouraging part-time
    • four-day working week
    • Advantages: lower carbon emissions, free people to engage in care/welfare-improving activities, stabilize employment, less necessary production declines
  • Enable sustainable development:
    • cancelling unfair/unpayable debts of low- and middle-income countries
    • curbing unequal exchange in international trade
    • creating conditions for productive capacity to be reoriented towards achieving social objectives

Examples

  • Free helath care/education: many european nations guarantee it
  • high-quality public housing: Vienna, Singapore
  • basic incomes/shorter working hours: Finland, Sweden, New Zealand

Polls

  • prioritize well-being and ecological objectives over growth: Europe
  • job guarantee and working-time reduction: United States, United Kingdom

Learn

  • From countries which had to adpat to low-growth conditions: Cuba, Soviet Union, Japan

Financing

Subsidies

  • stop subsidising fossil-fuel extraction

taxes

  • ecologically damaging industries: air travel, meat production
  • wealth: increase public resources, reduce inequality

Risks

Inflation

Manage them through

  • progessive taxation
  • encouraging
    • shared/collaborative consumption
    • renovation and repair
  • support community-based servies

issue currency

It can increase payments on government debt.
Manage it by careful coordinated fiscal (tax) and monetary policies.

Reshape provisioning systems

  • Bottom-up studies suggest, that better provisioning system could deliver decent living standards with much less energy than today, but don't fully account for institutions as the state.
  • [Top down studies](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0959378021000662?via%3Dihub
  • ) suggest more energy is needed to meet human need. They factor in institutions like the state but also wasteful consumption like yachts.
