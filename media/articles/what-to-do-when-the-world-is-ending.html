Summary/First paragraph

Answer: Face our despair and choose action e.g. building movements.
Why?: Because we don't know how it will end and what else is possible.

Special, but not that special

Ok. It’s time to move on. It’s ok to be sad, it’s ok to be angry. But this happens to many people, this and much worse. You’re special, but not that special. Eventually you have to accept this and adjust.

In this case, "this" is referred to loosing a baby, but it can be thought of any (really) bad event.

world is ending - nothing new

the experience of the world ending is not new

E.g. due to a prophecy or a real threat, many ancestors also experienced "the end of the world"-feeling. In many cases their worlds did end e.g. the arrival of Columbus or the destruction of Hiroshima. So history is not very positive. But their also is an interesting question: What do people do when their worlds are ending?
In these moments a lot of people do very brave things. Now another questions arises: Why bother if the world was going to end anyway?

Why bother?

  • Actions are remembered: To inspire other people.
  • Meaning: e.g. to meet with another human again, fight for a "greater" future

    I can let the newspapers tell me how my life will go, or I can decide for myself.

    • George Lakey

Despair is easy

People have always been good at imagining the end of the world, which is much easier to picture than the strange sidelong paths of change in a world without end.

  • Rebecca Solnit writes in Hope in the Dark

Despair is the easy way out. Imagining and fighting for a change in our world is harder. Despair is a self-fulfilling prophecy. We want to be true, so we don't take action and therefor nothing changes and it e.g. "world is ending" really happens.

How should we know!?

Despair is also bad politics. But that doesn't have to stay like that. And how should our tiny human brains know, how everything will happen?

Get together!

Instead of thinking, we already know how it will end, we should take action:

  • join powerful social movements
  • build institutions that provide masses of people with a vehicle for belonging, meaning, and long-term struggle.
  • being authentically alive on this planet in a time of collapse
    This requires
  • good strategy, healthy groups able to wield it, and a movement with a culture open and creative and compelling enough to win over the enormous numbers of people necessary for real transformation.
  • humility — about what’s possible, about ourselves, about each other.

    Wherever human beings are, we at least have a chance because we’re not only disasters; we’re also miracles.

    • James Baldwin
