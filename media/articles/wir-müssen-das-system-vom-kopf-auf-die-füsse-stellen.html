Interest increase

Main part of todays inflation is caused by concerns and speculators increasing its margins dramatically. Especially the energy concerns, after the russian attack, which are affecting all other products.

  • key interest rate increase is wrong answer to todays inflation
    • key interest rate increase would be for an inflation caused by more demand than supply

Attack on «normal people»

Attack on people renewing their mortgages and loans.
Central banks wants that less is consumed and invested, to decrease the demand. Would be okay with an inflation caused by demand. But this inflation is profit-driven. Inflation may still fall due to the reduction in purchasing power - but at immense social cost.

Class struggle

Some even demand that unemployment should raise to 6%. So the capital pressures the workers. In the beginning of the pandemic governments were willing to rescue lives with a lot of money. That gave workers more power. Not the central bank want to raise unemployment on purpose again and decrease worker's power.

G7 and Global South

Interest rate increase damages Global South (again). G7 never cared about the rest of the world.
Now investments flows back to the «secure» places like Europe, USA or Japan. Currencies of poor countries increases and they cannot pay back their debts of their interests. Additionally import of fuel and nutrition gets more expensive.

Cancel debt

At minimum debts of the Global South should be canceled.
Debt relief is normal in capitalism: Banks cancel huge sums of debts for corporations each year.

Neoliberalism

  • critisied that Global South suffers under it
  • but economist Branko Milanovic showed, that in the Global South a middle class was created.
    Capitalism has different faces. The neoliberal era has delayed multiple positive effects e.g. life expectancy: Increase the most after the second world war and flattened in recent decades. And the biggest increase was in China, which didn't follow the International monetary fund guidelines.

India

10 year Boom in India from 2002 was based on international capital and the mining of resources e.g. minerals or wood. That is what India is exporting, besides IT stuff, which isn't sustainable. In addition just the richet 5% profited from that. So no gain for the majority: India is a bipolar economy.

Narendra Modi

He pushes an aggressive patriarchic nationalism against Muslims. In the favor of some friendly capitalists.
It's working because the government controls the states institutions and social media. The democratic instutions is broken right now.

What Economy

GDP doesn't represent progress (in various areas). Progress and investments in e.g. education should be without questions what that means for the economy.
We have to ask what economy fulfills our aims. We have to change the system for that.
How?

  • stronger regulations e.g. for every dollar investement in fossil energies, invest 5 dollars in renewable energies
  • expand public investments: green energies, energetic sanitation, health, education, care (such technologies won't be replaced by new technologies)
    - ease unemployment problem
    Money from
  • greater taxes for rich and multinational concerns

Switzerland still a tax heaven

  • OECD tax reform has been watered down enormously to minimum company tax of 15% even though us financial minister proposed 21%
    - compared to tax heaven Irland with 12.5%
    Switzerland has advocated 12.5% and now the parliament even wants to pay subsidies for concerns paying extra taxes => a very unsocial behaviour

Worldwide

There is progress with more open talk about industrial politics, green transformation and necessity of more social security.
