Richest 10% are key to climate

  • world’s richest 10%, most of the middle classes in developed countries ( anyone paid more than about $40,000 (£32,000) a year) => responsible for half of all global emissions
    • #disclaimerN So probably all of Swiss people

Difference in a country, not between nations

  • beginning (1990s, start of climate negotiations) most inequality in carbon emissions where between nations => three decades later, most inequality is between rich and poor within countries

“It’s very important to stress that big gaps between countries still remain. But on top of this you now also have a lot of within-country inequality"

  • e.g. IEA data shows that the poorest 10% in the US still have a footprint bigger than 90% of those in India.

  • in all cases: richest 10% cause as much as poorest 50%

    • USA/China: even more than poorest 70%
    • Central Africa: even as much as remaining 90%

  • key factors/differences are

    • transportation: 20-40x higher than poorest 10%
    • USA/Canada: 1/3 of emissions of richest 10% caused through traffic and as much as poorest 70%
    • consumption: 20-50x higher

If you just focus on the average, you’re missing a big part of the problem and you might also miss the right policy"
- said Dr Lucas Chancel, a co-director of the World Inequality Lab at the Paris School of Economics, whose team have tracked the rise of carbon inequality within countries.

Thus carbon emission taxes can be

  • useless e.g. when people have to pay more, but they really need cars bc. they live in rural places
  • or even backfire e.g. "gilets jaunes"

It will not be enough to just impose taxes on carbon, as the rich can essentially afford to pay the extra amount, while poor people would be more affected.

Solution: Tax the rich

  • policy sticks e.g. taxation only on those, who have capacity for it
  • policy carrots e.g. subsidies for those who are unfairly burned by rising fuel and food prices

Millionaires growth, budget loss

  • amount of millionaires likely to grow from 51 million (2021) to 511 million (2050) (taking inflation into account) => that alone could wipe the remaining carbon budget of 250bn tonnes

Tax little, raise much

a recent report, noted that a “relatively modest” progressive wealth tax of 1.5% on people with $100m or more in assets – 0.001% of the global population – would raise $295bn a year. That is a similar amount to what is needed to protect the world’s people from the growing impacts of the climate crisis.

Important!

Rich lifestyles can change to reduce emissions without damaging wellbeing. What matters most to people is our relationships with others and our ability to be social, and those things aren’t carbon intensive to enjoy or maintain.
