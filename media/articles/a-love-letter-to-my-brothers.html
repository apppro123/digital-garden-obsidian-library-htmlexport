Summary/First paragraph

Text on patriarchy.

Word patriarchy

Some people suggest words like "domination system", "culture of dominatin" or "culture of exploitation".
That doesn't work, because they don't work on a material level. Instead patriarchy is connected to "paternity", which is a relationsship that happens on the material, biological plane and isn't an automatic given.

Paternety

Long period of material dependence on others.
We are entirely dependent on a one-sided giving to be able to survive the first few years of our life, and we continue to be dependent on love for the entirety of our life

Mothering

Mothering, in this context, means an orientation to others’ needs.

Start of patriarchy

Suggest massive collective trauma from physical and climatic catastrophes as the mechanism of the initial tansitions to patriarchy. So they lost trust in life and turned away from living based on orienting to needs. This leads to a drive of hoarding and accumulating.
Patriarchy became meaningful, when it becomes important who someone's child is in order to pass on to them what has been accumulated.

Fathering

It's clear who is the mother, when a baby is born, but there is no simple way (absend modern DNA reserarch) to know who the father is unless women are controlled. Patriarchy isn't about man, it's about fathering. Patriarchy emerged from turning away from life and aiming to control the processes of life, not women. But controlling women became necessary, to protect the invading group.
After the trauma of an invasion, the women became a threat. They were a link to what came before, to what the incoming invades had lost and came to fear, a symbol of all they couldn't trust any longer. From then on, women's work, experience, wisdom and knowledge, ways of being, need and contributions have been devalued. Example in Europe are times of persecution of women as witches.

Patriarchy state

Every patriarchal state has pushed outward in an endless cycle of finding new resources — minerals, food, and people — to sustain the practices of the ruling classes and their protectors, the armies and the police, who need to be fed and clothed even while not producing anything on their own.

Winners!

There is no way to “win” against patriarchy, because against-ness, in itself, is a patriarchal orientation.
I have said, already, that I don’t see us ever fighting patriarchy and winning, nor do I want to win, because then there are, again, losers.

There is no men vs women

When it comes to patriarchy in particular, all of us enact patriarchal patterns to various degrees.

Solution

  • bring in enough love, to subvert the logic of separation
  • to eradicate extreme poverty, eradicate extreme wealth
  • an island of love, an island of commitment to liberation for all, without any exceptions

Poverty

Poverty is a result and impoverishment the material process that creates it.

Impoverishment

It begins with accumulation. This has two consequences: Few having too much while many have too little. Over time leading to a big difference.

War

From early on, war has had everything to do with women.

Europe

In village after village

  • killed man
  • incorporated women
    to preserve the new male line required controlling the women.

Capitalism

I have always sensed that there must be violence inherent to capitalism, because I couldn’t imagine why, otherwise, the vast number of people whose work lives are one endless, unremitting misery would choose to continue doing this work, day after day.

Capitalism was from the beginning a multi-regional phenomenon in which some people in some regions gain material wealth at the expense of most poeple in the same region and at immense cost to almost all people in other regions (and to all life forms and nature)

The Doctrine of Discovery

which gave Portugal and Spain the authority to take over any land not previously inhabited by or claimed by Christians and to use what's on it and subjugate any people on it.
Still being used today in Canada and US by governments and corporations when encroaching on indigenous populations.

justification of cruelty

While exploitation and impoverishment existed and continue to exist within Europe, the degree of violence that Europe brought to other places could only be justified by making the target populations less than human. In the beginning because they were not Christians, then because the looked different e.g. darker skin colour. The result of all this is that the vast majority of the world’s poor are dark-skinned.

Poor white people

Also often peasant of the Middle Ages in Europe struggled. But they lived in communites where they could feed and clothe themselves without needing to buy things.
Once forced off the land, they were reduced to making themselves available to work for wages.
The majority of those living below the poverty line in the US are white. Generally people of Eruopean descent, anywhere in the world, have significant material benefits in relation to the groups they have colonized. But there is also a significant number of people of European descent, both in Europe and at least in the English-speaking former colonies, who are seriously not doing well. An Example is the phenomenon "deaths of despair" (drugs, alcohol, suicide) in the ranks of low-earning whites.
